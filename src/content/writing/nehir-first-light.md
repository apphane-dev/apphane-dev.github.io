Nehir is the first room in the house to switch its light on. This placeholder
stands in for the kind of note we'll write when a project ships something worth
trying: a short account of **what changed, why it matters, and where to try it** —
never a raw changelog.

## What changed

An update answers a reader who has thirty seconds. Lead with the one thing they
can now do that they couldn't before, then give them the command.

```sh
brew install --cask guria/tap/nehir
```

## Why it matters

Scrolling columns keep every window at the size you gave it. Instead of tiles
shrinking to fit, the workspace grows sideways and you move along the river. That
is the whole thesis of Nehir, and the reason it stays one layout model rather
than a window manager for every taste.

## Where to try it

Grab a release, read the config guide, and file friction in Discussions. Updates
like this one link straight back to the room they came from.
